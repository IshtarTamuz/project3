Seven Steps

The group coaching process has seven steps:

  1. Select the case-giver and the time keeper
  2. Intention statement by the case-giver
  3. Stillness and deep listening
  4. Mirroring
  5. Generative dialogue
  6. Closing remarks
  7. Journaling

To get a full understanding of each of these steps watch this video.

Focus point 1: How to make a strong intention statement

As you learned from the video, the case-giver is the person who shares a problem or a challenge they would like help with from the group . To present the case with clarity, answer the following five questions:

  • What is my key challenge or problem I'm trying to solve?
  • Who else is affected by this challenge?
  • What is the future that I'm trying to create?
  • What do I need to let go of, and what do I need to learn?
  • What do I need help with?

Focus point 2: How to practice deep listening

Deep listening means listening with all of our senses. Specifically, it means that:

  • You are asking yourself, "what do I hear," "what do I feel," and "what are the sensations that are going through my body while I'm receicing the information?
  • You are focusing on what trully resonates with you. What metaphors and images does the information evoque?
  • You share your feelings, the metaphors and images that came up.
  • You resist the temptation to give advice or to try to solve the case-giver's problem.
